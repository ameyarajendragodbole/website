Biography

Ameya Rajendra Godbole is a Mechanical Engineer and researcher specializing in robotics, physical modeling, and control systems. Currently, he is working as a Senior Quality Engineer with the Physical Modeling group at MathWorks Inc., where he focuses on Simscape Multibody, a leading simulation tool for modeling and analyzing 3D mechanical systems such as robotics, automotive suspensions, and aerospace mechanisms. His work ensures the accuracy, reliability, and performance of physics-based simulations, helping engineers design and validate complex mechanical systems with confidence.

Ameya holds a PhD and M.S in Mechanical Engineering from The University of Texas at Arlington, where his research explored mathematical modeling and nonlinear control of UAVs with cable suspended payloads, UAS reachability analysis, and trajectory design for autonomous systems. He worked as a Research Student at the Aerospace Systems Laboratory (ASL), University of Texas at Arlington under the guidance of Dr. Kamesh Subbarao. Ameya's dissertation research was funded by National Science Foundation under the project "S&AS:FND: Safe Task-Aware Autonomous Resilient Systems (STAARS)" and award number 1724248. He is also certified in Applied Data Science: Leveraging AI for Effective Decision-Making from MIT Professional Education.

Previously he worked as a Physical Modeling Quality Engineering Intern at the MathWorks Inc. from January 2017 to August 2017.

Interests: Unmanned Vehicle Systems, Robotics, Mathematical Modeling and Simulation of Dynamic Systems, Control System Design, State and Parameter Estimation, System Identification, AI.

Research

The advancements in sensor technology and increase in onboard computation power has seen a large increase in the use of UAS platforms. This has provided an opportunity to deploy aerial vehicles for surveillance, search and rescue operations in disaster situations like floods and earthquakes. Given the advantages like hovering at a certain location, agile mobility, and vertical take-off and landing capability, the multirotor vehicles have proven to be useful for payload delivery when the drop-off location of the payload is critical.

The payload transportation using multicopter unmanned aerial vehicles can be useful in a variety of applications like construction, and repair of structures at high altitudes or operating in difficult-to-access, remote, or hazardous locations for sample delivery or retrieval problems. Additionally, a multi-agent system consisting of multiple vehicles can be deployed for transporting heavier payloads.

The payload transportation problem using multicopters can be addressed using robotic manipulators attached to the aerial vehicle or through the use of cable to carry the payload. Although the use of multicopters with manipulators provides the advantage of the payload being rigidly connected to the body of the vehicle, it also increases the overall inertia of the system, thus limiting the maximum weight of the payload it can lift. Thus, the use of a cable suspended payload is an alternative solution proposed to solve this problem.

The multicopter with cable suspended payload is a coupled underactuated dynamical system. Thus, it is necessary to design a controller to attenuate the oscillations of the payload while trying to maintain the desired trajectory (position, velocity, and acceleration) of the multicopter for the overall safe operation. Additionally, the control framework should be capable of rejecting external disturbances like drag force due to the wind induced on the system.

The solution to this control problem is presented in a couple of ways. The first solution to this problem is presented through the design of an extended state observer-based active disturbance rejection controller. The disturbance induced in the system due to the motion of the payload is estimated using the extended state observer with the position of the quadcopter as the input to the observer. Using these disturbance estimates, an active disturbance rejection controller is designed to achieve favorable performance of the quadcopter.

The second solution to the control problem is presented using an energy-based approach called passivity-based control that asymptotically stabilizes the swing of the payload during transportation. The implementation of the passivity-based controller requires the knowledge of higher time derivatives of the payload oscillations. Assuming only the swing angles of the payload with respect to an Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) are measured, these states (primarily the angular velocity) are estimated using a continuous-discrete Kalman filter.

The framework will be extended for the multi-agent system of quadcopters carrying a cable suspended payload.
